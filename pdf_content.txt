Introduction
●
Low Engagement: Traditional text-based surveys often see response rates 
drop below 25% due to survey fatigue¹.
●
Sentiment Complexity: Over 60% of unstructured feedback contains multiple 
opposing sentiments within a single utterance².
●
Analytical Limitation: Single-dimensional scores fail to capture these 
nuances, masking actionable insights.
●
Data Density: Multimodal (voice) inputs yield 3x more descriptive data than 
typed text, requiring robust ingestion pipelines³.
●
Proposed Solution: An end-to-end AI architecture for parsing code-mixed 
feedback and extracting granular insights via Aspect-Based Sentiment 
Analysis (ABSA).
¹ Nulty (2008) ² Kastrati et al. (2020) ³ Ruan et al. (2018); Martin (2020)

--- Page 5 ---
Existing System
●
Existing text-based mechanisms classify entire paragraphs simply as positive, negative, 
or neutral, completely missing complex, multi-aspect opinions.
●
Standard English-centric NLP models face severe degradation when processing 
code-mixed (e.g., Hinglish) conversational feedback natively used by students in India.
●
Current institutional tools are purely reactive; they do not autonomously suggest 
pedagogical or infrastructural interventions based on identified issues.
●
Administrators must manually read through thousands of surveys and execute complex 
database queries to extract actionable insights, creating a massive operational 
bottleneck.

--- Page 6 ---
Identiﬁcation of problem
●
The Multimodal Gap: Vast majority of current feedback systems rely entirely on pre-typed text, 
completely ignoring unstructured, multilingual voice inputs.
●
The Analytical Gap: Without Dependency Graphs and Aspect-Based Sentiment Analysis 
(ABSA), systems fail to disambiguate contradictory sentiments applied to specific operational 
aspects.
●
The Proactive Gap: Current applications of Generative AI in education are largely used as 
reactive chatbots, rather than autonomous, threshold-triggered curriculum intervention 
mechanisms.
●
The Novel Intervention: This project introduces Code-Mixed ABSA and Confidence-Aware 
Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) to actively solve these specific institutional bottlenecks.

Summary of Literature Review
●
BiLSTM / RNN based ABSA (2024): Evaluates large-scale student 
feedback but struggles with sentence-level contradictory aspect sentiments.
●
Data Augmentation for ABSA (2025): Improves model accuracy but is 
restricted exclusively to high-resource English typed text.
●
Graph Neural Networks / GNNs (2025): Maps syntactical dependencies to 
extract precise targets but is computationally heavy for real-time inference.
●
Retrieval-Augmented Generation (2026): Grounds LLMs in proprietary 
data but is used strictly as reactive Q&A chatbots, lacking proactive anomaly 
triggers.

Problem Deﬁnition
Standard Natural Language Processing approaches to feedback 
analysis are insufficient for actionable institutional change. They 
lack the ability to handle mixed-language audio, fail to disambiguate 
opposing sentiments applied to specific operational aspects, and 
require intensive manual data mining. These critical gaps prevent 
timely, automated pedagogical interventions and limit the efficacy of 
quality assurance in modern educational institutions.

--- Page 11 ---
Objectives
●
To engineer a fault-tolerant Voice Ingestion Pipeline utilizing state-of-the-art STT models to 
transcribe and standardize code-mixed student feedback.
●
To implement Aspect-Based Sentiment Analysis (ABSA) integrated with Dependency 
Graphs to extract specific entities and map granular sentiment polarities.
●
To develop an automated curriculum intervention mechanism using a Confidence-Aware 
Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG) architecture grounded in university syllabi.
●
To design a Text-to-SQL Semantic Parsing engine that allows administrators to generate 
dynamic visual dashboards using natural language queries.

Proposed tools and techniques
●
Audio Ingestion: OpenAI Whisper for robust Multilingual and Code-Mixed 
Speech-to-Text conversion.
●
NLP Parsing: HuggingFace Tokenizers, SpaCy/Stanza for Dependency Graphing and 
morphological grounding.
●
Knowledge Base: ChromaDB or Pinecone for storing dense vector embeddings of 
institutional syllabi and guidelines.
●
Backend Infrastructure: FastAPI (Python) implementing asynchronous background 
workers to prevent inference bottlenecking.
●
Dynamic Frontend: React.js equipped with Plotly for rendering JSON payloads into 
interactive visual administrative dashboards.

Expected Outcomes
●
A Scalable Multimodal Ingestion Framework: A robust, fault-tolerant system capable of transcribing 
and standardizing code-mixed student feedback (audio and text) into a structured format, overcoming the 
limitations of traditional, low-engagement surveys.
●
High-Resolution Diagnostic Intelligence: A sophisticated Aspect-Based Sentiment Analysis (ABSA) 
engine that utilizes dependency-aware parsing to map granular polarities to specific academic and 
infrastructural entities, replacing flawed single-dimensional sentiment scores.
●
Grounded Curriculum Decision Support: An automated intervention mechanism that generates 
data-driven pedagogical recommendations, strictly anchored to university syllabi via a Confidence-Aware 
RAG architecture to ensure institutional alignment and accuracy.
●
Natural Language Administrative Interface: A Text-to-SQL semantic parsing engine that democratizes 
data access, enabling non-technical administrators to generate dynamic, real-time visual dashboards and 
complex reports using natural language queries.

Conclusion
●
This study establishes a proactive, automated approach to institutional quality 
assurance, replacing outdated reactive survey methods.
●
Highlights the critical need for Code-Mixed ABSA and Confidence-Aware RAG 
when evaluating authentic student feedback in the Indian educational landscape.
●
Delivers a practical, interactive administrative tool alongside clear, actionable 
curriculum insights derived from granular semantic parsing.
●
Contributes toward modernizing educational intelligence from passive observation 
to generative, data-driven intervention.
●
Opens promising avenues for integrating Continuous Reinforcement Learning 
(RLHF) directly into institutional administrative workflows.
